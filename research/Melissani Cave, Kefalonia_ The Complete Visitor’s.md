# Melissani Cave, Kefalonia: The Complete Visitor’s Guide

## Overview

**Melissani Cave** (Greek: Λιμνοσπήλαιο Μελισσάνης, pronounced "Leemno-SPEE-leo Meh-lee-SAH-nees") is one of Greece’s most breathtaking natural wonders, located on the island of Kefalonia near the village of Karavomylos and just minutes from Sami. Famous for its sunlit underground lake, mythological history, and unique boat tours, Melissani is a must-see for any visitor to the Ionian Islands[^1][^2][^3].

## About the Cave

- **Type:** Limestone cave with an underground lake
- **Dimensions:** Approx. 160m long, 40m wide, 36m high; lake depth up to 30m[^4]
- **Discovery:** Rediscovered in 1951 by Giannis and Anna Petrocheilos; opened to the public in 1963[^5][^6]
- **Mythology:** Named after the nymph Melissanthi (Μελισσάνθη), who, according to legend, drowned herself here after unrequited love for the god Pan. The cave is also called the "Cave of the Nymphs" (Σπήλαιο των Νυμφών)[^7][^8][^9][^10].


## Getting There

- **Location:** 2 km northwest of Sami, near Karavomylos village, Kefalonia[^2][^3][^10]
- **By Car:** Well-signposted from Sami and Agia Efimia. Ample parking is available next to the entrance[^2][^11][^12].
- **By Bus:** Local KTEL buses run from Argostoli and other major towns, especially in summer[^2].
- **By Taxi:** Taxis are familiar with the route; expect a 40-minute drive from Argostoli[^13][^14].
- **Tours:** Many organized tours include Melissani and nearby Drogarati Cave.

## The Experience

- **Access:** Walk down a short, steep tunnel with handrails to the lake[^5].
- **Boat Tour:** Small rowboats (up to 12 people) take you through two chambers: one sunlit, one darker with impressive stalactites[^1][^23][^10].
- **Duration:** 10–15 minutes per boat ride.
- **Photography:** Best between 11:30–14:00 when sunlight creates magical blue reflections[^1][^24][^25][^4].
- **Swimming:** Not permitted, to protect the ecosystem[^21][^22].
- **Facilities:** Parking, small café/kiosk, toilets at the entrance[^2][^26].


## Best Time to Visit

- **Midday (11:30–14:00):** Sunlight streams through the collapsed roof, illuminating the turquoise water for the most spectacular photos[^1][^24][^25][^4].
- **Avoiding Crowds:** Arrive early morning or late afternoon, and avoid cruise ship days for shorter queues[^27][^2][^12].


## History \& Mythology

- **Myth:** The nymph Melissanthi, heartbroken by Pan, drowned herself in the lake. The cave was a place of worship for Pan and the nymphs, as evidenced by archaeological finds[^7][^8][^6][^28][^9][^10].
- **Archaeology:** Excavations in 1962 uncovered a clay figurine of Pan and plates depicting dancing nymphs (now in the Argostoli Archaeological Museum)[^5][^8][^6][^10].
- **Geology:** The cave formed by the collapse of limestone, with brackish water mixing seawater and freshwater. The 1953 earthquake enlarged the roof opening, creating today’s iconic sunlit effect[^4][^10].


## Practical Information

### What to Wear

- **Comfortable shoes:** The path is short but steep and can be slippery[^27][^5].
- **Light clothing:** The cave is cool, but the area outside can be hot in summer.
- **Bring a light jacket** if visiting in spring or autumn.


### Accessibility

- **Mobility:** The descent is steep; not suitable for wheelchairs or those with significant mobility issues[^27][^5].
- **Children:** Safe and enjoyable for families; children under 6 enter free[^19][^20].


### Dining

- **On-site:** Small café/kiosk for drinks and snacks[^2][^26].
- **Nearby:** Sami and Karavomylos offer tavernas and restaurants with local cuisine.


### Map \& Contact

- **Coordinates:** 38.256944°N, 20.623611°E[^3][^29]


## Where to Stay

- **Sami:** Closest town, with hotels, guesthouses, and apartments.
- **Agia Efimia:** Picturesque harbor village, 10 minutes’ drive.
- **Argostoli:** The island’s capital, about 40 minutes by car, with a wide range of accommodation.


## Nearby Sights \& Activities

- **Drogarati Cave:** Impressive stalactite cave, 10 minutes’ drive; combo ticket available[^30][^31][^21][^22].
- **Myrtos Beach:** One of Greece’s most famous beaches, 20 minutes by car.
- **Antisamos Beach:** Beautiful, organized beach near Sami.
- **Karavomylos Lake:** Small, scenic lake next to the cave.
- **Sami Village:** Traditional harbor, restaurants, and shops.


## Weather

- **Summer (May–Oct):** Hot, dry, and sunny; ideal for visiting.
- **Spring/Autumn:** Mild, fewer crowds.
- **Winter:** Limited opening hours; check before visiting[^24][^15].


## Nightlife

- **Sami and Agia Efimia:** Relaxed bars, tavernas, and occasional live music.
- **Argostoli:** Livelier nightlife, clubs, and late-night venues.


## Tips for Visitors

- **Arrive early** to avoid queues, especially in July–August[^27][^2][^12].
- **Bring cash** for tickets and tips for boatmen.
- **Photography:** The guides are happy to take photos for you.
- **Respect the site:** No swimming, littering, or touching the cave formations[^24].


## Greek Information \& References

- **Λιμνοσπήλαιο Μελισσάνης**: Εντυπωσιακό γεωλογικό φαινόμενο, με ιστορία που συνδέεται με τη νύμφη Μελισσάνθη και τον θεό Πάνα. Αρχαιολογικά ευρήματα του 3ου και 4ου αιώνα π.Χ. αποδεικνύουν τη λατρεία του Πάνα και των νυμφών στο σπήλαιο[^7][^8][^6][^28][^9].
